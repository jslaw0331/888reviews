How to play baccarat: a beginner’s guide

Baccarat is one of the easiest casino games to learn. You place a bet on Player, Banker, or Tie — the dealer handles the rest. This guide breaks down card values, main bets, and your first round step by step.

01

The goal of the game

Predict which hand totals closest to 9 — Player or Banker. You are not playing the cards yourself; you simply choose where to bet. If your side wins, you are paid according to the bet type.

02

Baccarat card values

Aces count as 1; cards 2–9 are face value; 10s and face cards count as 0.

  • 7 + 6 = 13 → counts as 3
  • 9 + 5 = 14 → counts as 4
  • 8 + 8 = 16 → counts as 6

When a total exceeds 9, only the last digit counts — keeping every hand between 0 and 9.

03

Understanding the three main bets

Player bet

  • Payout: 1:1 (no commission)
  • House edge: ~1.24%

Banker bet

  • Payout: 1:1 minus 5% commission
  • House edge: ~1.06% — best long-term choice

Tie bet

  • Payout: 8:1
  • House edge: ~14.36% — avoid for regular play

Tip: Stick to Player and Banker bets; use Tie only for entertainment.
